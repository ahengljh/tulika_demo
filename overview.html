Project Objectives

Breakthrough in powerful edge computing through synergistic cross-layer approach

50X Efficiency Goal
Server-Class on Edge

The overall objective of the PACE project is to deliver highly efficient software programmable accelerators based on Coarse-Grained Reconfigurable Architecture (CGRA) that can offer near ASIC-like compute efficiency (i.e., performance-per-watt) while allowing full software programmability. The concrete goal is to bring server-class processing capability to resource-scarce edge devices at ultra-low power through a synergistic cross-layer approach. We expect to reach up to 50× improvement in compute efficiency compared to general-purpose processors in state-of-the-art edge devices. We will realize this breakthrough in powerful edge computing by adopting an end-to-end approach with leading-edge innovations spanning applications, algorithms, compiler, architecture, and circuits.

Completed

PACE 1.0: Silicon-Proven Success

The key deliverable of the project is the PACE 1.0 accelerator chip with specifications of 500 GOPS/W and 1hr battery life at weight of 1kg. We have successfully achieved this goal. The PACE 1.0 chip was sent for fabrication in December 2023 and the PACE accelerator chip that was sent out for fabrication in Dec 2022 has been tested and measured.

The PACE CGRA can run at a max frequency of 105 MHz while only consuming 43mW at 1V supply (100% PE utilization), achieving a maximum energy efficiency of 360 GOPS/W at 0.6V and 21MHz. When process normalization is considered, PACE 1.0 offers a peak energy efficiency of 673 GOPS/W and ~1090 GOPS/W when normalized to 28nm and 22nm processes respectively.

PACE 2.0: Next-Generation Design

PACE 2.0 represents a significant evolution, targeting 2000 GOPS/W efficiency through advanced SIMD architecture with 8-lane floating-point operations and integrated Address Generation Units. Designed in 12nm technology, it achieves 38% improvement over state-of-the-art in normalized efficiency.

We have moved to 12nm for better efficiency and achieved ~2 TOPS/W in simulations by employing SIMD architecture with support for floating point operations for quantized AI workloads. The PE now supports 8-bit eight lane SIMD operations, increasing data-width from 16bit to 64bit, with each PE performing eight parallel 8-bit floating point operations for peak throughput of 0.512 TOPS.

Open Source

Morpher: World-Class Compiler Framework

Our Morpher compiler is the best in class in the world in generating quality mapping, achieving fast compilation time, offering flexibility to explore different architectural designs, and providing end-to-end design flow. Morpher is an open-source integrated compilation and simulation framework that can assist design space exploration and application-level developments of CGRA based systems.

Made available as open-source in December 2022, various research groups across the world have reached out for collaborations on this toolchain. Morpher achieves 13× faster compilation compared to state-of-the-art approaches while maintaining quality mappings through techniques like PANORAMA, HiMap, and LISA.

WalkingWizard: Real-World Application

The PACE 1.0 chip has been successfully integrated with WalkingWizard, a truly wearable EEG headset designed for everyday use. WalkingWizard integrates dry electrodes with flexible PCB assembly to achieve accurate EEG sensing even under motion.

By incorporating the PACE 1.0 chip, we improve the real-time processing capabilities of WalkingWizard, enhancing its performance in capturing and analyzing EEG signals during activities such as walking or engaging in VR environments. This integration facilitates development of advanced applications like real-time emotion monitoring and cognitive state assessment, ultimately pushing the boundaries of mobile brain-computer interfaces and making EEG technology more accessible for everyday use.

Integration Benefits

  • Real-Time Processing: On-device signal processing for emotion monitoring and cognitive state assessment
  • Mobile BCI: Advanced applications for VR interaction and assistive technologies
  • Extended Operation: PACE's 43mW power consumption enables all-day wearable use
  • Motion Tolerance: Maintains accurate sensing during walking, running, or VR activities
