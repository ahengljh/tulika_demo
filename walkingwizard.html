WalkingWizard EEG System

PACE-Powered Wearable Brain-Computer Interface

Successfully Integrated with PACE 1.0

Revolutionary Wearable EEG System

We have leveraged the PACE 1.0 accelerator chip to enhance the performance of EEG devices. The PACE 1.0 chip aligns well with the demands of wearable EEG systems, providing the necessary computational power while maintaining portability and energy efficiency.

WalkingWizard, a truly wearable EEG headset designed for everyday use, integrates dry electrodes with flexible PCB assembly to achieve accurate EEG sensing even under motion. By incorporating the PACE 1.0 chip, we aim to improve the real-time processing capabilities of WalkingWizard, enhancing its performance in capturing and analyzing EEG signals during various activities, such as walking or engaging in VR environments.

Integration Goals

  • Real-Time Processing: On-device signal processing enabling immediate feedback for emotion monitoring and cognitive state assessment
  • Mobile Brain-Computer Interfaces: Facilitates development of advanced applications like real-time BCI control for VR interaction and assistive technologies
  • Extended Battery Life: PACE's ultra-low power consumption (43mW) enables all-day wearable operation
  • Everyday Use: Pushing boundaries of mobile BCI, making EEG technology more accessible and practical for everyday use outside laboratory settings
System Integration: We have mapped the EEG application on the PACE 1.0 accelerator chip and designed the new PCB system to integrate the chip in the WalkingWizard platform

Flexible PCB Assembly

The WalkingWizard system features a flexible PCB assembly that integrates dry electrodes with the signal processing circuitry. This flexible design is crucial for achieving comfortable wear and maintaining signal quality during physical movement.

Design Features

  • Dry Electrode Technology: Eliminates need for conductive gel, enabling quick setup and removal without skin preparation
  • Flexible Substrate: Conforms to head shape for comfortable extended wear and maintains electrode contact during motion
  • Integrated Signal Processing: On-board amplification and digitization before PACE accelerator processing
  • Motion-Resilient Design: Maintains accurate EEG sensing even during walking, running, or VR interaction
  • Compact Form Factor: Integrated design minimizes size and weight for true wearability
Electrode Type: Dry (no gel required)
PCB Type: Flexible substrate
Channels: Multiple EEG channels
Processing: PACE 1.0 accelerated

Real-World Usage Scenarios

WalkingWizard is designed for everyday use in natural environments, enabling EEG data collection and brain-computer interface applications outside traditional laboratory settings. The system has been tested and validated in various real-world scenarios.

Application Scenarios

  • Desktop Computing: Cognitive load monitoring and focus tracking during work or study sessions, enabling productivity optimization
  • VR Environments: Real-time BCI control in virtual reality applications, including gaming, training simulations, and therapeutic applications
  • Physical Activity: EEG monitoring during walking, light exercise, or daily activities for research and health monitoring
  • Research Applications: Mobile neuroscience studies investigating brain activity in natural settings rather than constrained laboratory environments
Real-Time Processing: PACE 1.0's 360 GOPS/W efficiency enables continuous on-device signal processing with minimal battery drain, supporting hours of continuous operation

Technical Implementation

The WalkingWizard system integrates multiple technological innovations to achieve its goals of wearability, accuracy, and real-time processing. The flexible PCB assembly houses the dry electrodes, signal acquisition circuitry, and interfaces with the PACE 1.0 accelerator for on-device processing.

System Architecture

  • Multi-Channel Acquisition: Multiple dry electrodes positioned according to 10-20 international system for comprehensive brain coverage
  • Low-Noise Amplification: High-gain, low-noise amplifiers for each channel to capture weak EEG signals (typically 10-100 µV)
  • ADC Conversion: High-resolution analog-to-digital conversion preserving signal fidelity
  • PACE Accelerator Integration: Mapped EEG signal processing kernels onto PACE 1.0 for real-time filtering, artifact removal, and feature extraction
  • Wireless Transmission: Processed results transmitted wirelessly for visualization and further analysis
