WalkingWizard EEG Integration

Real-World Brain-Computer Interface Application

Successfully Integrated

Revolutionary Wearable EEG System

The PACE 1.0 chip has been successfully integrated with WalkingWizard, a truly wearable EEG headset designed for everyday use. This demonstrates PACE's capability in real-world edge computing applications.

Making EEG Technology Accessible

WalkingWizard integrates dry electrodes with flexible PCB assembly to achieve accurate EEG sensing even under motion, pushing the boundaries of mobile brain-computer interfaces and making EEG technology practical for everyday use.

Integration Benefits

By incorporating the PACE 1.0 chip, WalkingWizard achieves improved real-time processing capabilities that enable advanced applications:

Real-time Emotion Monitoring

Continuous tracking of emotional states through EEG signal analysis, enabling responsive applications for mental health and wellness.

Cognitive State Assessment

Real-time evaluation of attention, focus, and cognitive load for applications in education, work productivity, and research.

Brain-Computer Interfaces

Advanced BCI applications including gaming, device control, and assistive technologies for individuals with disabilities.

Mobile EEG Analysis

On-device processing enables EEG data collection and analysis in natural environments outside traditional laboratory settings.

Technical Implementation

System Architecture

The team has successfully mapped EEG signal processing applications onto the PACE 1.0 accelerator and designed a new PCB system to integrate the chip into the WalkingWizard platform.

Impact and Future Directions

The successful integration of PACE 1.0 with WalkingWizard demonstrates the practical applicability of our CGRA technology in challenging real-world scenarios. This achievement validates our approach of co-designing hardware and applications for edge computing.

Future Enhancements

  • Integration with PACE 2.0 for enhanced processing
  • Advanced machine learning models for better classification
  • Multi-modal sensing combining EEG with other biosignals
  • Expanded application domains in healthcare and research
