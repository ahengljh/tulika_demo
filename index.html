About

I am a Vice Provost (Academic Affairs) and Provost's Chair Professor of Computer Science at the National University of Singapore (NUS).

My research focuses on the design of embedded computing systems with a special emphasis on energy-efficient and real-time-constrained computations. I lead the Embedded COmputing (eCO) Lab at NUS School of Computing.

Research

Our research at the eCO Lab focuses on designing next-generation embedded and IoT systems that are energy-efficient, safe, and intelligent.

Current Projects

Software Support for Heterogeneous Multi-Cores in Dark Silicon Era

Developing compiler and runtime support for efficient execution on heterogeneous multi-core architectures, addressing power and thermal constraints in modern processors.

Many-Core System Design for 5G Base Stations

Designing specialized many-core systems optimized for 5G wireless communication infrastructure with real-time processing requirements.

Universal SoC: Automatic Generation of High-Performance SoCs for IoT

Creating flexible System-on-Chip architectures that can adapt to diverse IoT application requirements through automated design methodologies.

Research Tools

Chronos 2.0

Timing Analyzer for Embedded Software - A comprehensive tool for worst-case execution time analysis.

Lin-Analyzer

FPGA Accelerator Performance Analysis Tool - Automated performance modeling and optimization for FPGA-based accelerators.

oo7

Spectre Vulnerability Detection and Patching - Security analysis and mitigation for speculative execution vulnerabilities.

Computational Kernels for Wearables

Optimized algorithms and implementations for energy-constrained wearable computing platforms.
